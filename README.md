### Project Description
   When patients undergo brain imaging, one of the most important goals is determining whether a tumor is present and, if so, identifying its type as quickly and accurately as possible. Brain tumors such as glioma, meningioma, and pituitary tumors can have serious health consequences and often require timely diagnosis to guide treatment decisions. Traditionally, radiologists and physicians examine MRI scans manually to detect abnormalities and classify tumor types. Although medical professionals are highly skilled, manual interpretation can still be time-consuming and subject to human error, especially when large numbers of images must be reviewed. With the increasing availability of medical imaging data and advances in artificial intelligence, automated image classification systems can support clinicians by providing faster and more consistent tumor detection, ultimately improving diagnostic efficiency and patient outcomes.
   
In this project, a deep learning algorithm will be developed to classify oncology brain MRI images into four categories: glioma tumor, meningioma tumor, pituitary tumor, and no tumor. The dataset includes labeled brain MRI images for each of these classes, with diagnoses verified beforehand. By training the model on these labeled examples, it can learn to recognize visual patterns associated with each tumor type and distinguish them from healthy, no-tumor scans. Once trained, the model will be able to analyze a patient’s brain MRI image and predict the correct category, enabling fast and accurate classification of brain tumors.
