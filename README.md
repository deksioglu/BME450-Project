### Project Description
  When patients arrive at a doctor’s office, the emergency room, or in an ambulance, one of the most critical vital signs measured is the heart rate. An electrocardiogram (ECG) allows physicians to detect heart arrhythmias, irregular heartbeats that can indicate serious conditions, including heart attacks. Prompt and accurate diagnosis of these arrhythmias is essential to prevent severe complications. Currently, physicians must analyze ECG patterns manually, either on a screen or from printed charts. While healthcare professionals are highly trained, human interpretation carries the risk of error. Furthermore, with the rise of wearable health technologies, individuals can now monitor their heart activity continuously in everyday life. Early detection of arrhythmias through such technologies could provide timely intervention and prevent irreversible damage, improving patient outcomes significantly.

In this project, a deep learning algorithm will be developed that will be capable of diagnosing heart arrhythmias directly from ECG data. The dataset includes ECG images representing several arrhythmia types: atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter, ventricular tachycardia, bradycardia, and ST-segment elevation. Each category in the dataset contains pre-labeled ECG images that have been accurately diagnosed by experts. By training our model on these labeled examples, it can analyze a patient’s ECG image and correctly identify the corresponding cardiac arrhythmia, enabling fast and accurate detection.
